FAQ

You can hack in any classroom that is labeled as a Pearl Hacks hacking space and in any public spaces. Certain upstairs classrooms will be reserved for workshops during certain time periods. These classrooms will be labeled. Our main hacking space will be the Great Hall which is where the food, mentors and hardware will be located. During the day, feel free to move around our venue. However you will only be allowed to hack in the Great Hall or go offsite overnight.

Although staying for the entirety of the event is not mandatory we highly recommend you stay as long as you can to experience everything Pearl Hacks has to offer. Feel free to navigate the event as you see fit to create the best hackathon experience for yourself.

It’s totally okay! Hackathons are not meant to be competitive in nature. Creating a working project is fantastic but we care much more about whether or not you enjoyed your time here and learnt more about technology!

We encourage all participants to showcase what they have achieved throughout the weekend - even if the project is unfinished! Many of the demos don't have all the planned features worked out. The demo fair really is more about sharing your experience developing a new project. And besides, our lovely sponsors are offering a lot of prizes! With that said, project submission is definitely not mandatory. It's completely fine if you want to come and just network and attend workshops.

For help with logistics:
  • Message a director or the general channel on slack. Directors can be identified by a ‘(Director)’ tag in their name on Slack.
  • Visit the help desk (registration booth) to talk to a volunteer
For help with your project: We will be releasing a mentor help webapp to match teams with mentors. More information on that soon!
